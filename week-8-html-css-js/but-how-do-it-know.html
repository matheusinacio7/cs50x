But How do It Know?

The Basic Principles of Computers for Everyone
by J. Clark Scott

This is a delightful book for anyone interested in understanding how a computer works, down to the electricity and the (literal) bits.

It's also a very good reminder that computers are incredibly dumb, but also very fast and very reliable,
and it's our job as programmers to break down complex problems into simple steps so that these dumb, but fast and reliable machines can execute them for us.

For people studying programming, I also recommend that you literally do the circuits shown in the book, using something like hneemann's Digital
It's very fun to create your own digital RAM and CPU.
